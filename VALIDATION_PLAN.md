# Dental Clinic Validation Plan

Goal: validate whether small dental clinics care enough about missed WhatsApp leads to try or pay for a lightweight AI receptionist.

## Target Customer Profile

Start with 3-5 small dental clinics that:

- Receive appointment requests through WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, or SMS.
- Have a front desk team that gets busy during working hours.
- Miss messages after hours or on weekends.
- Offer services with strong patient intent, such as emergency visits, cleanings, whitening, implants, braces, or root canals.
- Do not already have a sophisticated CRM or chatbot workflow.

Best first targets:

- Independent dental clinics
- Clinics with 1-3 locations
- Clinics that actively post a WhatsApp number
- Clinics where the owner or office manager is reachable

## Outreach Message

Short version:

> Hi, I am testing a simple WhatsApp AI receptionist demo for dental clinics. It answers common patient questions, captures appointment requests, and shows the owner who needs follow-up. Could I show you a 3-minute demo and ask for feedback?

Spanish version:

> Hola, estoy probando un demo de recepcionista de IA por WhatsApp para clínicas dentales. Responde preguntas frecuentes, captura solicitudes de cita y muestra al dueño quién necesita seguimiento. ¿Podría enseñarte un demo de 3 minutos y pedirte feedback?

## Demo Flow

1. Open the landing page.
2. State the pain point: late replies lose patients.
3. Open a scenario:
   - Pricing: `/chat?scenario=pricing&lang=en`
   - Appointment: `/chat?scenario=appointment&lang=en`
   - Emergency: `/chat?scenario=emergency&lang=en`
   - Complex escalation: `/chat?scenario=complex&lang=en`
4. Click **Send scenario**.
5. For appointment scenarios, complete the lead fields.
6. Open `/dashboard?lang=en` or `/dashboard?lang=es`.
7. Show new leads, hot leads, human follow-up, common questions, and lead details.
8. End with: "This is not replacing your staff. It helps your team avoid missing patients while they are busy."

## Questions To Ask

- How many patient messages do you receive through WhatsApp each day?
- How many are answered late or after hours?
- Which questions do patients repeat most often?
- When someone asks for a cita, what information do you need before calling back?
- Who owns follow-up today?
- How do you know if a patient message was missed?
- Would a daily summary of new leads and follow-ups help?
- Which integration would matter first: WhatsApp, calendar, Google Sheets, or a dashboard?
- Would you test this with real patient messages if setup were simple?
- What would make this trustworthy enough for your clinic?

## Signals Of Interest

Strong signals:

- They mention missed WhatsApp messages as a real problem.
- They ask if it can work with their current WhatsApp number.
- They ask about price, setup time, or implementation.
- They want the dashboard or summary sent to staff.
- They want to try it with one real service, such as emergencies or whitening.
- They ask if it can book into their calendar.

Moderate signals:

- They like the concept but want a human to review all responses.
- They prefer Google Sheets or WhatsApp summaries before a dashboard.
- They ask for Spanish-first responses.

Weak signals:

- They do not use WhatsApp for leads.
- They already respond instantly with a dedicated team.
- They see no value in structured follow-up.
- They only care about a full production system before giving feedback.

## What To Measure

For each clinic, record:

- Estimated WhatsApp leads per week
- Estimated missed or late replies per week
- Most common patient questions
- Biggest follow-up pain
- Preferred first integration
- Willingness to pilot
- Willingness to pay
- Required trust/safety controls

## Build The Real Version When

Move beyond the static MVP if at least 2-3 clinics show strong interest and one clinic is willing to pilot.

The real version is justified when clinics confirm:

- Missed or late patient replies are common.
- Appointment requests need better capture.
- Staff would use a follow-up summary or dashboard.
- WhatsApp integration is important enough to test.
- A paid pilot is plausible if the system saves staff time or captures more appointments.
